Cheat Sheet for Prompt Engineers: Crafting Effective LLM Prompts

Crafting prompts for Large Language Models (LLMs) like GPT-3 or GPT-4 requires clarity, precision, and a bit of strategy. This cheat sheet provides guidelines and examples to help prompt engineers generate more effective queries and instructions.

1. Capitalization

  • Proper Nouns: Always capitalize names, titles, and specific locations.
    Example: "Generate a story about Alice in Wonderland."
  • Start of Sentences: Begin prompts with a capital letter for readability.
    Example: "Describe the process of photosynthesis."

2. Use of Special Characters

  • Quotation Marks (""): Use for exact phrases or terms.
    Example: "What does 'machine learning' mean?"
  • Parentheses ((): Provide additional context or clarification.
    Example: "Translate 'bonjour' (French) to English."
  • Bullet Points or Numbers: Organize lists or steps.
    Example: "List the steps to bake a cake: 1. Preheat the oven. 2. Mix ingredients."

3. Emphasis and Separation

  • Emphasis: Use _underscore_ or *asterisks* for emphasis in plain text.
    Example: "Explain the importance of _cybersecurity_."
  • Clarity and Separation: Use spaces and punctuation effectively to separate ideas.
    Example: "Compare and contrast: democracy vs. authoritarianism."

4. Syntax and Structure

  • Direct Instructions: Start with action verbs for clarity.
    Example: "Generate a title for an article about space exploration."
  • Question Form: Frame prompts as questions to seek specific information.
    Example: "How does quantum computing work?"

5. Explicit Instructions

  • Output Specifications: Clearly state any requirements for the response.
    Example: "Write a short story (under 300 words) about a time-traveling cat."
  • Style and Tone: Specify if a certain style or tone is desired.
    Example: "Explain the theory of relativity in a humorous tone."

6. Formatting for Readability

  • Paragraphs and Line Breaks: Use these to structure longer prompts or instructions.
    Example: "Describe the life cycle of a star. Include the following stages:
    - Nebula
    - Main Sequence
    - Red Giant
    - Supernova"
  • Feedback and Iteration: Use the model's responses to refine your prompts.
    Example: "Based on your previous answer, what are the ethical implications?"

Remember, the effectiveness of a prompt often hinges on how well it communicates the desired outcome to the model. Use these conventions to structure your prompts for clarity, specificity, and context, enhancing the likelihood of receiving the intended response.
